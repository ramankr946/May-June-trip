Cascais

27 May – 31 May · 4 nights · Your Portugal base

A beautiful seaside resort town 30km west of Lisbon — upmarket fishing village with a gorgeous bay, excellent seafood, and exactly the right pace for landing jet-lagged with a toddler. Your AIMA biometrics office is here, so zero commute on the appointment morning. Lisbon is 40 minutes by coastal train for day trips.

Where to stay

Cascais seafront / town centre area

Stay within 10 min walk of Cascais train station and the bay. The town centre is compact, flat, and walkable — ideal with a toddler. Avoid Estoril (too quiet) and the A5 corridor hotels (too far from the sea).
Sheraton Cascais Resort — Marriott Bonvoy Cat 6 · seafront · pool Bairro Art House — boutique, central Grande Real Villa Itália — seafront palace hotel
All options are within easy walking distance of the AIMA office and Cascais beach. Book seafront or sea-view rooms where possible.

Transport arranged — Biometrics Day (29 May)

Private car from hotel to AIMA Cascais office, departing 9:30am for 10:00am appointment. Return transfer arranged for ~11am. Fallback options if needed:

Private car — arrange via hotel concierge Bolt / Uber ~€8 10 min walk

Seville

31 May – 3 June · 3 nights · Andalusia's jewel

Grand Islamic palaces, orange-tree lined lanes, flamenco drifting from behind closed doors, and food that makes you want to stay another week. June heat enforces the perfect wind-down rhythm: out early, hard siesta, beautiful evenings.

Where to stay

Barrio Santa Cruz / Cathedral area

Stay in or immediately adjacent to the old town. Walking distance to Alcázar, Cathedral, and Santa Cruz means you're not battling taxis with a toddler in 35°C heat. The best hotels in Seville are concentrated here.
Marriott Bonvoy property — book via marriott.com for points
Avoid hotels across the river in Triana unless you specifically want that neighbourhood — adds unnecessary transfers with a toddler.

Málaga

3 – 5 June · 2 nights · Beach & culture

The most underrated city on this route. Beautiful old town, the world's finest Picasso collection, vibrant food scene, and a clean central beach 10 minutes from everything. More relaxed and less touristy than Seville. Zain gets his proper beach days here.

Where to stay

Port / Cathedral area (Centro Histórico)

Stay on or near the port (Muelle Uno) or within the historic centre. Walking distance to La Malagueta beach, Picasso Museum, and the Alcazaba. Avoid the seafront strip east of the beach — it's resort territory, not the real city.
Marriott Bonvoy property — book via marriott.com for points
Look for a hotel with a rooftop pool — particularly good for early evenings with Zain before dinner.

Granada

5 – 8 June · 3 nights · The centrepiece

The emotional peak of the trip. At 738m altitude it's cooler than Seville and Málaga — a genuine relief. The Alhambra is in a category of its own. The Albaicín is the most atmospheric urban neighbourhood in Andalusia. Free tapas with every drink at every bar.

🚨 Book Alhambra tickets NOW — alhambra-patronato.es

General admission with Nasrid Palaces sells out 4–6 weeks in advance in June. Get the 9:30am slot on 6 Jun. The Nasrid Palaces are the critical ticket. Do this today — everything else can wait.

Where to stay

Plaza Nueva / Cathedral area

Stay in the historic centre within walking distance of Plaza Nueva — the gateway to both the Alhambra hill and the Albaicín. Avoid the Albaicín itself for accommodation with a toddler — the lanes are steep and impractical with luggage.
Marriott Bonvoy property — book via marriott.com for points
Worth checking if the Parador de Granada has availability — it sits inside the Alhambra grounds and is unlike anywhere else.

Madrid

8 – 11 June · 3 nights · Final city · Fly 12 Jun

A worthy final city. Three nights gives you Retiro Park for Zain, the Prado for the adults, and Madrid's exceptional food scene for the evenings. The best-fed city of the entire trip. End the journey properly here.

Where to stay

Jerónimos / Retiro / Paseo del Prado area

Stay in the cultural triangle between the Prado, Retiro Park, and Paseo del Prado. You're walking distance to everything on the itinerary and you avoid the noise of the Sol/Gran Vía tourist core. This is the right part of Madrid for a wind-down stay.
Marriott Bonvoy property — book via marriott.com for points
Choose a hotel in the Jerónimos / Retiro area — walking distance to everything on the itinerary and away from the noisy Sol tourist core.
